JA Washington Event Visual Design

How can we encourage community members to donate to a cause?

Context

Design Like Mad is a 12 hour design hackathon where students and professionals can work together to help design solutions for non-profit organizations.

Here is me

Research

When we met with our non-profit representative, she showed us examples of the event's design from previous years. As a team, we picked out which features seemed successful, and which features could be improved upon.

We also made sure to clearly understand the goals of the organization and the goals of the event.

  • Get more money

    • Ideation

    With a good understanding of JA Washington's goals, we started brainstorming themes for the event visuals.

    Since the event is held at Safeco Field, we considered having a baseball theme.

    Ultimately, we decided to use a ribbon motif that I had designed to symbolize the growth that children will experience thanks to JA Washington.

    Designing for a 11,245 sq-ft screen

    My two main tasks were to design

  • The auction board
  • The jumbotron banners that would display the auction items

    • The auction board wasn't too difficult, but when it came to the jumbotron, it was literally a big endeavor. I had to take into account the fact that there would technically be three different screens to design on. I decided that the center box should be for the images, while the left and right boxes would be for mainly text.

    Challenges

    The 12 hours went by quick. Despite the limitation of time, our team could have done this a bit differently.

    One of the biggest challenges was creating unity between the four of us. Not everyone had the same version of Adobe InDesign, so it took a bit of time to get all of the assets to match on all of our designs.
