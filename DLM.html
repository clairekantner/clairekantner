JA Washington Event Visual Design

How can we encourage community members to donate to a cause?

Timespan

12 Hour Design Sprint, November 2017

Task

Create an identity for JA Washington's event: "Dare to Dream".

Teammates

Akiko Masker, Amber Kim, Brittany Resch

Toolbox
  • Adobe Indesign

    • Context

    Design Like Mad is a 12 hour design hackathon where students and professionals can work together to help design solutions for non-profit organizations.

    JA Washington is a non-profit group dedicated to giving K-12 students the skills they need to be economically successful in the future.

    Lynn providing feedback midway through the sprint

    Battle Plan

    When we met with our non-profit representative, Lynn, she showed us examples of the event's design from previous years. As a team, we picked out which features seemed successful, and which features could be improved upon.

    We also made sure to clearly understand the goals of the organization and the goals of the event.

  • Increase donations
  • Appeal to all members of the Seattle community, and not just regular donors

    • To achieve these goals, Lynn wanted us to create an identity that is clean and modern, but still upholding the underlying message of Junior Acheivement: helping kids reach their full potential.

    Ideation

    With a good understanding of JA Washington's goals, we started brainstorming themes for the event visuals.

    Since the event is held at Safeco Field, we considered having a baseball theme.

    Ultimately, we decided to use a flying ribbon motif to symbolize the growth and learning JA brings to classrooms.

    Ideating potential directions

    Designing for an 11,245ft screen on my tiny laptop.

    Challenges

    My two main tasks were to design

  • The auction board
  • The jumbotron banners that would display the auction items

    • The auction board wasn't too difficult, but when it came to the jumbotron, it was literally a big endeavor. I had to take into account the fact that there would technically be three different screens to design on. I decided that the center box should be for the images, while the left and right boxes would be for mainly text.

    Space economy was an important factor in my design, as the jumbotron needed to accomodate a changing body of potential event sponsors, auction items, and other content.

    Working together with my team for 12 hours straight

    Takeaways

    The 12 hours went by quick. Despite the limitation of time, our team could have done this a bit differently.

    One of the biggest challenges was creating unity between the four of us. Not everyone had the same version of Adobe InDesign, so it took a bit of time to get all of the assets to match on all of our designs.
