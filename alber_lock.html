Alber-lock

UI Animation

Timespan

2 Week Project, Nov 2017

Task

Demonstrate Albers Color Principle through an interactive or animated experience.

Toolbox
  • Usability testing
  • Principle
  • Marvel App

    • Context

    For my Color and Composition class, I was given 2 weeks to create something that demonstrates Albers' color principle.

    The illusion of a single color appearing to be different depending on its background is the underlying theme of the principle. My task was to showcase this illusion through an interaction or motion.

    Process

    I decided to create an animated lockscreen concept because it integrates easy interactions, like tapping and swiping, which people are already familiar with. A lockscreen is something people interact with everyday. I wanted to make this everyday experience into something more entertaining, and the color illusion would be a great opportunity for this.

    Sketches to explore ways to represent Alber's color principle through the tapping interaction.

    Marvel Pop Prototype to show early concept

    Prototype Testing

    Using Marvel Pop and Principle, I created several low fidelity prototypes to test two key questions:

  • Does the product successfully implement "smooshing", and Albers' color principle?
  • Does the product feel resolved, and natural to use?

    • To make this project a success, I needed to make sure to implement the feedback from my users.

    Letting my classmates test the screens and provide feedback

    Key Findings

    My users revealed useful information which had led to a better informed design. Insights that drove my design included:

  • Consider the direction that the elements move. For one of my prototypes, the blocks would move left and right, rather than up and down, which confused one of my testers.
  • Consider the context in which the user would be unlocking their phone: one of my users reminded me that although fancy animation is fun to look at, her goal is to simply unlock her phone.

    • Accounting for so many possible button combinations was a nightmare

    Challenges

    The difficulty of prototyping a lock-screen is the number of potential combinations that the user could input. With so many possible button combinations, I had to figure out how to create a prototype that could demonstrate my concept. Ultimately, I decided that the demo's unlock animation could only be activated when the user inputs the number 7 as the last number.


    Takeaways

    Principle was difficult to pick up at first, and especially with the limit of time, I found it hard to move from one check-point to the next.

    Next time, I would...

  • Create a more complex animation, and test out Principle's other features, such as drivers, to increase the overall fidelity.
  • Represent the color principle in a more interesting way, as I had explored with my sketches, rather than through abstract squares.
  • Spend more time learning different shortcuts and tricks in Principle to increase my workflow
  • Slow down the animations a bit. Although unlocking a phone is a quick interaction, the illusion might be easier to experience if it was slower.
